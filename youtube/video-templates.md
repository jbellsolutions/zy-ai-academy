## Thumbnail Text Overlay Guidelines

### General Rules:
1. Maximum 3-5 words on the thumbnail
2. Font: Bold sans-serif (Montserrat Black, Impact, or Anton)
3. Text must be readable at phone-screen size (think: can you read it as a tiny rectangle?)
4. One main idea per thumbnail -- don't try to say everything
5. Use CAPS for impact words
6. Never put text in the bottom-right corner (YouTube timestamp covers it)

### Color Palette:
| Use | Color | Hex |
|---|---|---|
| Primary accent | Teal | #00BFA6 |
| Secondary accent / CTA | Orange | #FF6B35 |
| Background / text | Dark Navy | #1A1A2E |
| Clean text | White | #FFFFFF |
| Warning / attention | Yellow | #FFD700 |
| Positive results | Green | #4CAF50 |

### Text Overlay Examples by Video Type:

**Real Sessions:**
- "AGE 5" (with arrow)
- "40 PROBLEMS"
- "REAL SESSION"
- "NOT SCRIPTED"

**How-To Tutorials:**
- "FREE SETUP"
- "STEP BY STEP"
- "EXACT PROMPTS"
- "5 MINUTES"

**Results/Updates:**
- "6 MONTHS LATER"
- "IT WORKED"
- "REAL RESULTS"
- "MONTH 1"

**Thought Leadership:**
- "SCHOOLS ARE WRONG"
- "THEY MISSED THIS"
- "UNPOPULAR TAKE"
- "STOP DOING THIS"

### Layout Principles:
- Text goes on the left or top -- faces/action go on the right or bottom
- Use a semi-transparent dark box behind text if the background is busy
- Include a subtle brand element (small teal bar, logo watermark) in one corner
- High contrast always -- squint test: if you can't read it squinting, increase contrast

### What NOT to Do:
- No child faces (safety policy)
- No clickbait that doesn't deliver (builds distrust)
- No more than 2 colors of text
- No tiny text or paragraphs
- No generic stock photos
- No logos that take up more than 10% of the thumbnail
