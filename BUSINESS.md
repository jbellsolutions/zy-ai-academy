# Zy AI Academy — Business Foundation

## Mission

We help kids learn the way they actually learn — with AI that adapts to them, not a classroom designed in 1850.

Zy AI Academy exists for two reasons:

1. **Free families from a broken system.** The traditional school system was built for factories — sit still, follow instructions, memorize, repeat. It prepares kids for a future that no longer exists. College and traditional education are relics of a dying system. We believe every kid deserves personalized 1-on-1 education that meets them where they are.

2. **Teach kids the value of value creation.** This business is Zion's first business. Through it, he learns what school never teaches — how money works, how businesses run, how to create something that helps people, and how to think independently.

## Vision

A world where every kid gets a 1-on-1 education personalized to them — and where kids grow up understanding how to create value, not just consume it.

We're building toward a future where AI makes world-class education accessible to every family, and where kids learn that they can build, earn, and contribute from the very start.

## Our Story

Zy AI Academy was born when a dad watched his 5-year-old son struggle with basic addition.

Justin Bellware is a software engineer who saw that the traditional classroom wasn't working for his son, Zion. Instead of accepting it, he built something better — a set of AI-powered tutors that adapt to Zion's pace, meet him where he is, and make learning feel like play, not punishment.

The results changed everything. Zion went from struggling with basic math to completing 40+ problems daily, reaching 3rd-grade math at age 5, and reading at a 9-10 year-old level. He does it every morning before breakfast — not because anyone makes him, but because he wants to.

Other parents started asking how. So Justin and Zion turned it into a business — Zion's first business.

**Justin built the technology.** The AI tutors, the system, the infrastructure.

**Zion proved it works.** Every tutor was tested by him. Every feature got his real feedback. His results are the proof — unscripted, unedited, real.

**Now they run it together.** Zion is the founder, the product tester, the brand ambassador. He earns a real paycheck for real work. He's learning what business is, how money works, and what it means to help people — while helping families across the country break free from an outdated education model.

This isn't a cute story about a kid with a lemonade stand. This is a real business, built by a real kid's experience, solving a real problem.

## Financial Structure

### Revenue Split (on Net Revenue)

Net revenue = gross revenue minus all business expenses (payment processing fees, hosting, tools, marketing costs).

| Bucket | % of Net | Purpose | Details |
|--------|----------|---------|---------|
| **Investing** | 30% | Money that makes more money | Custodial accounts, index funds, assets that grow over time. Vehicles TBD. Managed by Justin with full transparency to Zion. |
| **Spending Cash** | 30% | Zion's paycheck | His to spend. Payment for his real contributions as product tester, ambassador, and content contributor. |
| **Savings** | 30% | Long-term security | Emergency fund, future opportunities, long-term wealth building. Untouchable for day-to-day spending. |
| **Operations** | 10% | Business costs | Hosting, payment processing, tools, marketing, and any costs to keep the business running. |

### How It Works

1. **Revenue comes in** from product sales (Founding Family, VIP, etc.)
2. **Operations (10%) comes off first** — covers hosting, tools, payment fees, marketing
3. **Remaining 90% splits evenly three ways** into Zion's buckets:
   - 30% → Investing (money working for him)
   - 30% → Spending Cash (his paycheck)
   - 30% → Savings (long-term security)
4. **Zion receives a regular paycheck** from the spending cash bucket
5. **Investing and savings are managed by Justin** on Zion's behalf, with full transparency — Zion can see every dollar and where it goes

### The Lesson

This structure isn't just about money management. It teaches Zion:

- **Investing:** "If I give you $10 and you give me back $15 in 6 months — that's investing. Your money works for you while you sleep."
- **Spending:** "You earned this. You decide what to do with it. That's what a paycheck is."
- **Savings:** "This is money for future-you. It's there when you need it for something big."
- **Operations:** "Every business has costs. Before you can pay yourself, the business has to run."
