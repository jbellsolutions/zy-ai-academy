# Zy AI Academy - 7-Day Member Onboarding Flow

**Platform:** Skool.com
**Goal:** New member goes from sign-up to first AI tutoring win in 7 days
**Owner:** Justin Bellware

---

## Onboarding Overview

```
Day 0: Welcome DM + Setup Checklist
Day 1: Post Introduction
Day 2: Complete "Start Here" Module
Day 3: First AI Tutoring Session + Post First Win
Day 4: (Rest day - explore community)
Day 5: Attend Weekly Call or Watch Replay
Day 6: (Continue sessions - build the habit)
Day 7: Share Progress + Earn First Badge
```

**Success metric:** If a member posts a "win" by Day 3 and returns by Day 7, they're 80%+ likely to stay long-term. The entire onboarding is designed around getting to that first win fast.

---

## Day 0: Welcome DM + Setup Checklist

**Trigger:** Immediately after joining the community
**Automation:** Skool sends auto-DM (set up in Community Settings > Welcome Message)

### Welcome DM Template:

```
Hey [FIRST NAME]! Welcome to Zy AI Academy.

I'm Justin -- I'm the dad who built this whole thing after watching my 5-year-old son 
transform with AI tutoring. Really glad you're here.

Here's your quick-start checklist to get rolling:

Step 1: Post a quick intro in the community (who you are, your kid(s) ages, what 
you're hoping to get out of this). The other parents are great -- they'll welcome you.

Step 2: Head to the Classroom and start Module 1: "Start Here." It's 6 short lessons 
and takes about 20 minutes. By the end, you'll be ready for your first session.

Step 3: Run your first AI tutoring session with your child. Then come back and 
post about it in "Wins & Progress." Even if it was messy or short -- that counts.

That's it for the first few days. Don't overthink it. Just start.

If you get stuck on ANYTHING -- tech, prompts, your kid not cooperating -- post in 
"Setup Help" or DM me directly. I read everything.

Talk soon,
Justin
```

### Automation Notes:
- Skool has built-in welcome DM functionality (Settings > Welcome Message)
- Personalization: Skool auto-inserts first name
- This CANNOT be a multi-day automated sequence in Skool natively -- see Day 1+ for manual/workaround approach

**Trigger:** Follow-up nudge if they haven't posted yet (manual check or prompted by welcome DM)
**Who does it:** Justin or community manager checks new members daily

**Response Template (Justin replies publicly):**

```
[Personal comment based on their intro -- reference something specific they said].

Your next step: head to the Classroom and knock out Module 1: "Start Here." 
Takes about 20 minutes and gets you ready for your first session. You've got this!
```

**Points earned:** +5 for posting introduction

## Day 3: First AI Tutoring Session + Post First Win

**Trigger:** Day 3 after joining
**Goal:** Member runs their first session AND posts about it

This is the most important day. If they post a win on Day 3, retention jumps dramatically.

### Morning Nudge DM Template (send around 8 AM ET):

```
Hey [FIRST NAME] -- today's the day!

Here's your mission (takes 15-20 minutes):
1. Open ChatGPT
2. Paste the math tutor prompt from Module 2, Lesson 3 (or the one from Start Here)
3. Sit with [CHILD NAME] and let them try 5-10 problems
4. Come back to the community and tell us what happened

That's it. Even if they only did 3 problems. Even if they got frustrated. 
Even if YOU got frustrated. Post about it. We've all been there.

"Wins & Progress" is where the magic happens: [LINK]

Rooting for you!
```

### After They Post Their First Win:

**Response Template (Justin replies publicly -- make this warm and specific):**
```
LET'S GO, [FIRST NAME]! This is what it's all about.

[Specific comment about their post -- reference what their kid did, how many problems, 
what the reaction was, etc.]

This is exactly how it started for Zy. The first session is always a little rough 
around the edges, but you showed up and your kid showed up. That's the whole game.

Tomorrow, do it again. Same time, same spot. The habit is what makes this work. 
By the end of the week, you'll see a real difference.

Welcome to the crew.
```

**Points earned:** +10 for posting a win

### If They Haven't Posted by End of Day 3:

**Gentle DM:**
```
Hey [FIRST NAME] -- no pressure at all, but wanted to check in. Did you get a 
chance to try a session with [CHILD NAME]?

If something came up or you hit a snag, totally fine. Post in "Setup Help" and 
we'll get you sorted. Sometimes the tech part trips people up and that's normal.

And if life just got busy -- no worries. The system isn't going anywhere. 
Whenever you're ready.
```

## Day 4: Rest Day (Explore the Community)

**No DM sent.** Let them breathe.

If they're posting or engaging, reply to their posts. If they're quiet, that's fine.

### Optional Engagement (only if they're active):
- Like/react to any posts they make
- If they comment on someone else's post, acknowledge it
- If they ask a question in Setup Help, prioritize answering it quickly

## Day 6: Continue Sessions (Build the Habit)

**No DM sent unless they haven't done a session since Day 3.**

The goal on Day 6 is habit formation. If they did a session on Day 3, they should be on session 2, 3, or 4 by now.

### Only If No Activity Since Day 3:

```
Hey [FIRST NAME] -- just a quick nudge. The first session is the hardest part, 
and you already did it!

The secret sauce is consistency. Even 10 minutes a day makes a huge difference 
over a few weeks. Zy started with short sessions too.

If you need help tweaking the prompts or adjusting the difficulty for [CHILD NAME], 
post in Setup Help. That's what we're here for.
```

```
Your experience helps other families who are just starting. And honestly, 
I learn from every single post too.

you might be close to Level 2 (Getting Started). That unlocks the bonus

Justin
```

## Troubleshooting Common Onboarding Issues

**"I can't get ChatGPT to work right"**
- Direct them to Setup Help
- Offer to jump on a 5-minute screen share
- Check if they're using the free tier (works fine) vs paid

**"My kid doesn't want to do it"**
- Direct them to Module 2, Lesson 7 (Troubleshooting motivation)
- Share that Zy had rough days too -- it's normal
- Suggest starting with just 5 problems, not 40

**"I don't have time for this"**
- Emphasize: 15-20 minutes/day, once set up
- The first week is the hardest -- after that it becomes routine
- Point to Module 5 (Parent Dashboard) for efficiency tips

**"Is this safe for my kid?"**
- Direct them to Module 6, Lesson 5 (AI Safety Deep Dive)
- Remind them: parent is always present during sessions
- Share the safety settings from Module 1, Lesson 4

**No response to any DMs (ghost member)**
- Send one final check-in on Day 10
- If no response, stop messaging -- they'll come back when ready or they won't
- Never send more than 4 unreturned DMs total
