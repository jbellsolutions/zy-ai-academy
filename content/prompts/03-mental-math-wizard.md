# MENTAL MATH WIZARD — Full Product Documentation

## ENGINEERED FOR

Mental Math Wizard was built on the teaching methods of Arthur Benjamin, the world-renowned "Mathemagician" who performs staggering mental calculations in front of live audiences. This tutor does not just drill math facts — it teaches your child the *strategies* that make mental math possible. Complements and cross-anchoring, left-to-right calculation, friendly number techniques, and pattern recognition.

This tutor was designed to:

- **Teach mental math as a learnable skill**, not an innate talent
- **Follow a structured 6-month curriculum** from number bonds through four-digit mental operations
- **Introduce one concept at a time** with guided practice before solo application
- **Gate advancement behind mastery** — the child must pass the Wizard Test (4 out of 5) to move forward
- **Build speed and confidence** through warm-ups that reinforce prior concepts every session
- **Maintain engagement** through a wizard theme with stickers, trophies, ranks, and a visible Progress Scroll

The 10-15 minute session length was calibrated specifically for mental math — long enough to introduce and practice a concept, short enough to maintain the intense focus that mental calculation requires.

SESSION STRUCTURE (10-15 MINUTES)
═══════════════════════════════════════════

Every session follows this exact five-phase format:

--- PHASE 1: QUICK RECAP (1-2 min) ---
Briefly review the concept from the previous session.
- State the concept name and the key technique

## PARENT TIPS

1. **Do not let your child use paper.** The entire point is mental calculation. If your child reaches for a pencil, redirect them: "Remember, wizards use their brains, not their pens." If they truly cannot solve it mentally, the concept needs more practice — that is what the Wizard Test is designed to catch.

2. **Do not rush through failed Wizard Tests.** If your child fails a test, the tutor will repeat that concept next session. Trust the system. Mastery before progression is the entire philosophy.

3. **Practice outside sessions casually.** At the grocery store: "What's 47 plus 35 in your head?" In the car: "What's 9 times 8?" Real-world mental math reps accelerate the curriculum.

4. **Celebrate the Progress Scroll.** At the end of each week, look at where your child is on the curriculum together. "You're in Month 2 already — that's two-digit addition territory."

5. **This pairs perfectly with Math Lab.** Math Lab builds computational and written fluency. Mental Math Wizard builds speed and strategy. They reinforce each other — that is why the Zy AI Academy system includes both.

## TROUBLESHOOTING

| Problem | Solution |
|---|---|
| Tutor skips curriculum steps | Say: "What concept are we on in the curriculum? Show my Progress Scroll and continue from there." |
| Wizard Test is too easy | The curriculum gets harder fast. If your child is breezing through Month 1, add: "Skip to Month 2 — my child already knows number bonds and complements." |
| Tutor gives wrong mental math answers | Say: "That calculation is incorrect. Redo it step by step." Switch to a stronger model if persistent. |
| Child cannot explain the technique back | This means they are mimicking, not understanding. Tell the tutor: "Re-explain this technique with a simpler example." |
| Session runs over 15 minutes | The session structure is designed for 10-15 minutes. If it is running long, the child may be struggling — which is a signal to slow down, not speed up. |
| Tutor loses track of curriculum position | Start the session with: "My curriculum position is Month [X], Week [X], concept [name]. Continue from there." |
