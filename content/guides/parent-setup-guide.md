## The Daily Schedule

Your child's day is split into two types of sessions: Required and Elective.

### Required Tutors (Do These Every Day)

| Tutor | Time | What It Covers |
|---|---|---|
| Math Lab | 20-30 min | Core math skills: arithmetic, word problems, number sense, problem solving |
| Language Arts | 30-60 min | Reading comprehension, writing, grammar, vocabulary, spelling |

### Elective Tutors (Pick 2 Each Day)

| Tutor | Time | What It Covers |
|---|---|---|
| Mental Math Wizard | 10-15 min | Speed drills, mental calculation tricks, number fluency |
| Communication Tutor | 5-10 min | Public speaking, conversation skills, articulation, confidence |
| AI Adventure Tutor | 15-20 min | Interactive story-based learning, choose-your-own-adventure education |
| Spanish Tutor | 15-20 min | Conversational Spanish, vocabulary, pronunciation, cultural context |
| Creative Thinking | 10-20 min | Lateral thinking, brainstorming, invention challenges, creativity exercises |

### Total Daily Commitment

About **1 to 1.5 hours** per day, depending on which electives you choose and your child's pace.

### Recommended Session Structure

- **Morning session** (before or after breakfast): Required tutors -- Math Lab first, then Language Arts
- **Afternoon session** (after lunch or before dinner): 2 Elective tutors of your child's choice

Letting your child pick their own electives gives them ownership over their learning. That matters more than you think.

## How to Install Your AI Tutors

Each tutor comes as a "system prompt" -- a block of text that tells the AI how to behave. Here's how to set it up on each platform.

### ChatGPT (Recommended)

This is the best experience. Custom GPTs remember their instructions and you only set them up once.

1. Go to [chat.openai.com](https://chat.openai.com)
2. Click **Explore GPTs** in the left sidebar
3. Click **Create** in the top right
4. In the **Configure** tab:
   - **Name:** Enter the tutor name (e.g., "Zy Math Lab")
   - **Description:** One-line description (e.g., "Daily math tutor for kids")
   - **Instructions:** Paste the entire system prompt for that tutor
5. Set a conversation starter like "Let's start today's lesson!"
6. Click **Save** and choose "Only me" for privacy
7. Repeat for each tutor

Once saved, each tutor appears in your sidebar. Your child just clicks the tutor name to start a session.

### Claude

1. Go to [claude.ai](https://claude.ai)
2. Click **Start new project** (or open an existing project)
3. In the project settings, find **Custom Instructions**
4. Paste the entire system prompt for that tutor
5. Start a new conversation within that project

Note: Claude does not have persistent Custom GPTs like ChatGPT. You will use Projects to keep each tutor's instructions saved.

### Gemini

1. Go to [gemini.google.com](https://gemini.google.com)
2. Start a new chat
3. Paste the system prompt at the very beginning of the conversation, before your child's first message
4. The AI will follow those instructions for the rest of that conversation

Note: Gemini does not save system prompts between conversations. You will need to paste the prompt each time you start a new session, or save them as bookmarked text snippets for quick access.

### Free ChatGPT (No Subscription)

If you are using the free version of ChatGPT without a Plus subscription:

1. Go to [chat.openai.com](https://chat.openai.com)
2. Start a new conversation
3. Paste the system prompt as your first message
4. The AI will follow those instructions for the rest of that conversation
5. Start a new conversation and re-paste the prompt for each new session

This works perfectly fine. The only difference from the paid version is that you paste the prompt each time instead of saving it as a Custom GPT.

## Personalizing for Your Child

Every tutor prompt includes two placeholders you need to replace:

- **[CHILD_NAME]** -- Replace with your child's first name
- **[AGE]** -- Replace with your child's age

Use find-and-replace (Ctrl+H or Cmd+H) to swap these out before pasting.

### Adjusting Difficulty

Each prompt is designed to auto-adjust based on age, but you can also:

- Tell the tutor "Make it easier" or "Make it harder" during a session
- Add a note at the end of the prompt like: "Start at a 1st-grade level" or "This child is advanced -- start at 5th-grade level"
- For younger kids (5-6), add: "Use very simple language and short sentences"

## Running Your First Session

Here's what the first session looks like:

1. **Sit with your child.** For the first 2-3 sessions, be right there with them. You are not teaching -- you are showing them how to interact with the tutor.
2. **Open the tutor.** Start with Math Lab. It is the most structured and gives your child a clear win early.
3. **Let the AI lead.** The tutor will introduce itself, assess your child's level, and start with appropriate problems.
4. **Help with typing if needed.** Younger kids (5-7) will need you to type their answers. That is completely fine.
5. **Celebrate wins.** When the tutor gives praise, reinforce it. "See? You got that right!"
6. **Keep it short.** If your child is losing focus after 15 minutes, stop. A short good session beats a long frustrating one.

After 3-5 sessions, most kids can run sessions with minimal help. You will shift from sitting next to them to checking in periodically.

## Checking Their Work

The AI tutors provide instant feedback, but you should still review:

- **Math Lab:** Ask your child to show you 2-3 problems they solved. Check the work, not just the answer.
- **Language Arts:** Read what they wrote. Ask them to read a passage to you out loud.
- **Electives:** Ask "What did you learn today?" and listen to their answer.

You do not need to grade anything. The AI handles that. Your job is to stay aware of what your child is working on and where they might need extra support.

### Progress Tracking

Use the printable Progress Tracker (included in your download) to log:

- Which tutors they used each day
- How long each session lasted
- Any scores or completion notes
- Your own observations

## The Parent's Role

You are a **coach**, not a teacher.

The AI teaches the content. Your job is to:

- **Set the routine.** Same time, same place, every day.
- **Remove friction.** Have the device charged, tutors bookmarked, workspace ready.
- **Monitor engagement.** If they are bored, switch electives. If they are struggling, adjust difficulty.
- **Celebrate consistency.** Praise the habit, not just the results. "You showed up again today. That is what winners do."
- **Review weekly.** Spend 10 minutes each Sunday looking at what they did that week.

You do not need to understand the curriculum. You do not need to prepare lessons. You just need to make sure they show up.

## FAQ

**1. What age is this for?**
Ages 5 through 12. The tutors automatically adjust difficulty based on the age you set in the prompt.

**2. Do I need a ChatGPT Plus subscription?**
No. The system works on the free version of ChatGPT, Claude, or Gemini. A ChatGPT Plus subscription gives you the best experience (saved Custom GPTs), but it is not required.

**3. How long does setup take?**
About 15-20 minutes to set up all 7 tutors. You only do this once.

**4. Can my child use this independently?**
Kids ages 8 and up can usually run sessions on their own after the first week. Younger kids (5-7) will need a parent nearby for typing and guidance.

**5. What if my child gets bored with a tutor?**
Switch electives. The variety is there for a reason. Also try telling the tutor "Make this more fun" or "Add a game" -- the AI will adjust.

**6. Is this a replacement for school?**
It can supplement school or serve as a core part of a homeschool curriculum. Many families use it as an after-school enrichment program.

**7. What devices work?**
Any device with a web browser: laptop, tablet, phone, Chromebook. A laptop or tablet with a keyboard is ideal.

**8. Can I use this for multiple children?**
Yes. Create a separate set of tutors for each child with their own name and age. Each child gets their own personalized experience.

**9. How do I know it's working?**
Track progress with the included templates. You will see improvement in math speed, reading comprehension, and writing quality within 2-3 weeks of consistent daily use.
